    7: Explanations

    7.1: Default Dates

    Tournament organizers will set a "Default Date" for all matches. Default dates include a day and time, keep in mind the date and time will be different if you are playing a tournament that is organized outside of your region.

    Teams can still play on a different date if both teams agree, but not after the deadline for the match.

    Teams are only allowed to reschedule matches to a date within the tournament's duration.

    Note: Teams do NOT have to reschedule matches which have been given a Default Date.

    7.2: Medic Melee Only Fight

    A Medic Melee Only Fight can be used to settle arguments over map pick advantages and/or choice of sides (i.e. RED and BLU). Please only use a Medic Melee Only Fight to settle an argument when it is explicitly stated in the rules of the competition or advised by a League Admin.

    One Medic from each team participates in a melee fist fight. The winning team gets to decide which side to start on or who gets the map pick advantage in a situation where both teams are equally seeded. Only the stock Melee and it's reskins are allowed, unless both Medics are using the same melee unlock. Weapon-switching, buffs, healing of any kind, taunting, camping and/or fleeing is not allowed. Multiple Medics from each team are allowed as well if both teams agree.

    Note: It is not mandatory for a team's designated Medic player to be chosen for a Medic Melee Only Fight.

    7.3: Match Format

    Unless specified otherwise, all regular season matches played in Pass Time League will be a Best of 1 map format, and played with the pt_global_official config, which specifies a winlimit of 3. Matches are to be reported based on the rounds won, e.g:

    Team 1 3-2 Team 2

    For playoffs, all matches except Grand Finals will be Best of 3 maps, and played with the pt_global_official config, which specifies a winlimit of 3. Grand Finals is Best of 5 maps with the upper bracket team starting with a map advantage (Essentially, the upper bracket team starts out 1-0.) Matches are to be reported based on the rounds won, e.g:

    Map 1: Team 1 3-2 Team 2 Map 2: Team 1 2-3 Team 2 Map 3: Team 1 3-0 Team 2

    7.4: Picks/Bans System

    This system is used to decide maps for playoffs and tiebreaker matches when specified by the competition.

    For a Bo3 set (Typically Regular Playoffs):

    The higher seeded team will decide whether to be Team A or Team B.

    • Team A bans a map
    • Team B bans a map
    • Team B picks a map
    • Team A picks a map
    • Team A bans a map
    • Team B bans a map
    • Team A picks a map
    • Remaining map will not be played.

    For a Bo5 w/ Map Advantage set (Typically Grand Finals):

    The higher seeded team will decide whether to be Team A or Team B. (For Grand Finals, this is the Upper Bracket team)

    • Team A bans a map
    • Team B bans a map
    • Team A picks a map
    • Team B picks a map
    • Team A bans a map
    • Team B bans a map
    • Team A picks a map
    • Team B picks the remaining map

    Picks/Bans may be done via Discord or through match comms. Team leaders are expected to pay attention to both methods throughout the week regardless of which is used. It is courteous for team leaders to make some form of contact with the other team leader in the case they are not responding to match comms.

    In the event that one team reaches out to the other team to complete the map picks/bans, and the other team fails to pick or ban a map within the 72 hour pick/ban deadline, then the team that reached out will instead pick or ban a map for them. If the aforementioned team continues to not respond, the other team will choose all of the maps for the match. A map pool consisting of eight maps is assumed. If less maps are available, instructions on how to proceed will be given on the match page.

    Unless stated otherwise, assume that if a tournament is using this recommended ruleset, that the pick ban system works like this.
